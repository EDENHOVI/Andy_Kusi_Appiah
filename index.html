Full Teaching Philosophy

Human Geography has shaped my academic and personal journey. From high school through to university, I was drawn to understanding the intricate connections between people and their environments. My curiosity deepened as I engaged with local communities in research, embracing Elinor Ostrom’s idea of local people as custodians of their environments. Today, I teach and research with a deep commitment to listening closely to the lived experiences of those I study, especially in the political ecology of urban domestic water access.

My teaching is rooted in a student-centred, inclusive approach that encourages critical thinking and appreciation of human-environment relationships. Inspired by the exceptional educators in my life, I aim to create a supportive learning atmosphere where students can connect theory with their own experiences. I design my courses to foster synthesis of socio-spatial knowledge, and I strive to make the classroom a space where diverse perspectives are welcomed and valued.

I prioritize transparent and thoughtful assessment methods. By providing grading rubrics, templates, and examples, I help students feel confident and engaged. Assignments like the reading summary encourage both formal academic writing and creative integration of personal insights. Feedback is future-oriented and affirming, helping students grow intellectually. I also use small group discussions and peer interaction to build community, promote critical dialogue, and identify learning challenges, allowing me to adapt lessons in real time.

Caring is a core principle in my teaching. I work closely with accessibility services to ensure all students, regardless of ability, can participate fully. I reach out to those facing personal difficulties and offer flexible support when needed. Inviting guest speakers into the classroom brings real-world perspectives that enrich learning and encourage empathy. Ultimately, I strive to create a learning environment that is respectful, inclusive, and empowering for all students.
